Week 1 Guide

Wegovy Week 1 Meal Plan: What to Eat Your First Week

Your first week on Wegovy is about learning how your body responds to semaglutide. Here's a simple 7-day meal plan so you can focus on adjusting, not meal planning.

Updated April 2026 9 min read
Quick Answer

During Wegovy week 1 (0.25mg starting dose), eat small, protein-focused meals built around lean protein and gentle carbs. Your appetite will likely drop within the first day or two. The easiest week-1 foods are Greek yogurt, eggs, chicken breast, fish, cottage cheese, rice, toast, and bananas. Skip fried food, greasy meals, spicy dishes, large portions, and alcohol. Even when you're not hungry, eat at least 80g of protein daily to protect your muscles.

What Happens During Wegovy Week 1

Your first Wegovy injection is 0.25mg — the same starting dose as Ozempic. Wegovy is approved for weight management (not diabetes like Ozempic), but the active ingredient is identical: semaglutide. Week 1 feels the same on both medications.

Here's what most people notice in the first few days:

Your appetite disappears faster than you expect. Some people feel it within hours. Others wake up the next day and realize they forgot to eat breakfast. Semaglutide slows how quickly your stomach empties and tells your brain you're full. The result is you go from normal hunger to "I could skip this meal" almost overnight.

Food preferences shift. Sweet things taste too sweet. Greasy food suddenly seems unappealing. Some people describe their body sending clear signals about what it does and doesn't want. Not everyone gets this in week 1, but it's common enough that you should pay attention when certain foods start sounding wrong.

Mild nausea might show up. About 40-50% of people get some queasiness in week 1. At the 0.25mg dose, it's usually not severe — more like mild motion sickness or early pregnancy nausea. It tends to peak 1-2 days after your injection, then fade by day 4 or 5.

This is all normal. Wegovy is doing what it's designed to do. Your job this week is to eat enough protein to preserve your muscle, stay hydrated, and figure out which foods your stomach tolerates best.

GLP Flow tip: Inject on a Friday evening if you can. That puts your toughest days (Saturday-Sunday) on the weekend when you can take it easy, eat light, and not worry about being at work feeling off.

Your 7-Day Wegovy Week 1 Meal Plan

This plan starts the day after your first injection (day 1). Every meal includes at least 25g protein. Everything is gentle on your stomach. Nothing takes more than 15 minutes to make.

Day 1 — First Day After Injection

Breakfast: Greek Yogurt Bowl

1 cup plain Greek yogurt, 1/4 cup granola, handful of blueberries, drizzle of honey. (28g protein, 310 cal)

Lunch: Simple Chicken + Rice

3 oz grilled chicken breast, 1/2 cup white rice, steamed broccoli. Light soy sauce. (29g protein, 330 cal)

Dinner: Scrambled Eggs + Toast

3 eggs scrambled with butter, 1 slice whole wheat toast, sliced tomato. (21g protein, 320 cal)

You'll probably notice your appetite is lower today. Eat smaller portions if a full plate feels like too much.

Day 2 — Nausea Window Begins

Breakfast: Protein Smoothie

1 scoop vanilla protein powder, 1/2 banana, almond milk, ice. Sip slowly. (30g protein, 280 cal)

Lunch: Cottage Cheese + Crackers

1 cup cottage cheese, 6-8 whole wheat crackers, cucumber slices. (26g protein, 280 cal)

Dinner: Baked Fish

4 oz tilapia or cod, baked with lemon and dill. Side of steamed green beans. (28g protein, 220 cal)

This is often the roughest day for nausea. Cold or room-temperature foods usually go down easier than hot meals. Split meals into smaller portions if needed.

Day 3 — Feeling Better

Breakfast: Overnight Oats with Protein

1/2 cup oats, 1 scoop protein powder, almond milk, chia seeds, cinnamon. Prep the night before. (30g protein, 340 cal)

Lunch: Turkey + Avocado Wrap

3 oz deli turkey, 1/4 avocado, lettuce, tomato, whole wheat tortilla. (26g protein, 340 cal)

Dinner: Chicken Stir-Fry (light)

4 oz chicken breast, mixed vegetables, light teriyaki sauce, 1/2 cup rice. (32g protein, 380 cal)

Most people feel much better by day 3. Nausea should be mild or gone. Your appetite is still lower than normal, but food sounds more appealing.

Day 4 — Adjusting Well

Breakfast: Egg Muffin Cups (3)

Batch-prepped egg cups with spinach and cheese. Reheat 30 seconds in microwave. (30g protein, 280 cal)

Lunch: Tuna Salad Lettuce Wraps

1 can tuna, light mayo, diced celery, wrapped in butter lettuce leaves. (30g protein, 260 cal)

Dinner: Ground Turkey Tacos

4 oz seasoned ground turkey, corn tortillas, salsa, plain Greek yogurt instead of sour cream. (28g protein, 350 cal)

Day 5 — Finding Your Rhythm

Breakfast: Cottage Cheese + Fruit

1 cup cottage cheese, diced peach or pineapple, sprinkle of almonds. (27g protein, 250 cal)

Lunch: Chicken Caesar (light dressing)

4 oz grilled chicken, romaine, parmesan, 1 tbsp Caesar dressing, croutons. (34g protein, 360 cal)

Dinner: Salmon + Sweet Potato

4 oz baked salmon, 1/2 medium sweet potato, roasted asparagus. (30g protein, 380 cal)

Day 6 — Best Day of the Week

Breakfast: Peanut Butter Protein Shake

1 scoop chocolate protein, 1 tbsp peanut butter, banana, almond milk. (35g protein, 380 cal)

Lunch: Shrimp + Quinoa Bowl

4 oz shrimp, 1/2 cup quinoa, cucumber, cherry tomatoes, lemon vinaigrette. (28g protein, 340 cal)

Dinner: Baked Chicken Thigh + Vegetables

1 bone-in chicken thigh (skin removed), roasted zucchini and bell peppers. (28g protein, 320 cal)

Day 6 is usually when you feel most like yourself. Good day to meal prep for the coming week.

Day 7 — Day Before Next Injection

Breakfast: Smoked Salmon Toast

3 oz smoked salmon, cream cheese, capers, red onion, everything bagel seasoning on whole grain toast. (27g protein, 320 cal)

Lunch: Black Bean + Chicken Bowl

3 oz chicken, 1/2 cup black beans, rice, pico de gallo, lime. (32g protein, 380 cal)

Dinner: Turkey Meatballs + Marinara

4-5 turkey meatballs (ground turkey, breadcrumbs, egg, Italian seasoning), light marinara, small side of pasta or zucchini noodles. (30g protein, 360 cal)

Tomorrow is your second injection. Prep easy foods tonight — overnight oats, yogurt, protein shakes — so the tough days are easier.

Foods to Skip in Wegovy Week 1

Your stomach is adjusting to slower digestion. These foods make that transition harder:

Fried and greasy food. Fries, fried chicken, fast food burgers, anything deep-fried. Fat naturally slows digestion. Wegovy slows it even more. Together, they create a backed-up feeling that can last hours. It's not worth it.

Heavily spiced dishes. Your GI system is more sensitive right now. That spicy curry you normally love might not agree with you this week. Dial back the heat until you know how your body responds.

Large portions. Even healthy food becomes a problem when you eat too much. Your brain is getting different fullness signals than it used to. A normal-sized meal from last week will feel like way too much this week. Start with half your usual portion and wait 20 minutes before going back for more.

Sugary drinks. Soda, juice, sweet tea. Empty calories that spike your blood sugar — the opposite of what semaglutide is trying to do. Stick to water, unsweetened tea, or black coffee.

Alcohol. Skip it for week 1 at least. Wegovy can make alcohol hit harder and faster than you're used to. Many people report getting intoxicated on less alcohol than normal. Plus it can worsen nausea. Wait until you know how your body responds.

GLP Flow tip: Use the "two-bite rule" in week 1. Before committing to a full meal, take two bites and wait 5 minutes. If your stomach feels fine, keep eating slowly. If it protests, switch to a cold/bland backup option. You're learning your body's new signals — don't ignore them.

The Protein Priority: More Important Than Calories

You'll hear a lot about calorie counting when you start Wegovy. Forget about it for now. Your appetite is going to naturally reduce your calories — that's the medication working. You don't need to restrict on top of that.

What you do need to track is protein. Here's why:

When you lose weight quickly (and many people drop 3-5 lbs in week 1, mostly water), your body breaks down muscle for energy unless you give it enough protein to prevent that. Research on GLP-1 weight loss shows that without intentional protein intake, up to 40% of weight lost can come from lean mass. That's the exact opposite of what you want.

The goal: 80-100g of protein per day, minimum. Across 3 meals, that's roughly 25-35g per meal. Every meal in this plan hits that range.

If you're struggling to hit that number, protein shakes are your best friend. A quick shake adds 25-30g in under a minute, even on mornings when chewing food sounds unappealing.

Hydration in Week 1: The Thing Everyone Forgets

Wegovy reduces your appetite for food. It does nothing to reduce your need for water. Many side effects people blame on the medication — headaches, fatigue, constipation, dizziness — are actually just dehydration.

Aim for at least 64 oz of water daily. More if you drink coffee (it's a diuretic). Practical tips: keep a water bottle at your desk and finish it twice before lunch, set a phone reminder every 2 hours, or add lemon or cucumber if plain water is boring.

Electrolytes help too. When you eat less, you take in fewer natural electrolytes from food. A sugar-free electrolyte drink or a pinch of salt in your water can prevent that drained, foggy feeling that hits many people in the first week.

Frequently Asked Questions

What should I eat the first week on Wegovy?

Focus on gentle, protein-rich foods that are easy to digest. Greek yogurt, eggs, chicken, fish, and cottage cheese work well during week 1. Avoid greasy, fried, or heavily spiced foods — they can trigger nausea on the 0.25mg starting dose. Eat small portions slowly, and stop when you feel satisfied rather than full.

Is nausea normal during Wegovy week 1?

Yes, about 40-50% of people experience mild nausea during the first week on Wegovy. At the 0.25mg starting dose, it's usually manageable — often worse 1-2 days after injection and improving by day 4. Cold foods, small meals, and avoiding greasy dishes help. Most people find nausea improves with each weekly dose.

How much protein should I eat on Wegovy week 1?

Aim for 80-100g of protein daily, spread across your meals. That's about 25-30g per meal. When your appetite drops on Wegovy, your body can break down muscle for energy unless you give it enough protein. Protein shakes, Greek yogurt, eggs, and lean meats help you hit this target even when you're not hungry.

Will I lose weight during Wegovy week 1?

Many people lose 2-5 pounds in week 1, though most of that is water weight. The 0.25mg starting dose is low enough that dramatic weight loss isn't typical yet. Wegovy's weight loss effect builds gradually as your dose increases over the first few months. Week 1 is about adjustment, not dramatic results.

Can I eat normally on Wegovy week 1?

Your definition of 'normal' will change quickly. Even at the low starting dose, most people find their appetite drops significantly within 24-48 hours. You'll likely eat smaller portions than before without trying. Listen to your body — when you feel satisfied, stop eating. Forcing yourself to finish meals can lead to uncomfortable fullness.
