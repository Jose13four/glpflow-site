What to Eat When Wegovy Kills Your Appetite

10 small, protein-packed meals designed for days when nothing sounds good. Because skipping meals on semaglutide isn't the flex you think it is.

Updated April 2026 8 min read
Quick Answer

When Wegovy suppresses your appetite, focus on small, protein-first meals every 3-4 hours. Greek yogurt, cottage cheese, protein shakes, and egg-based snacks are your best options — they pack 20-30g protein into portions small enough to eat without forcing it. Don't skip meals. Your muscles need fuel even when your brain says you're not hungry.

Why Appetite Loss on Wegovy Is a Double-Edged Sword

The reduced appetite is the point. That's literally why you're taking Wegovy — semaglutide tells your brain you're full, so you eat less, and you lose weight. It works. Sometimes a little too well.

The problem shows up about 2-3 weeks in. You realize you've eaten 600 calories by 6 PM and feel completely fine. You might even feel a little proud of that. But here's what nobody tells you in the prescriber's office: undereating on Wegovy is almost as common a problem as overeating was before it.

When you consistently eat too little, your body starts breaking down muscle for energy. Studies on GLP-1 medications show that without adequate protein, up to 40% of the weight you lose can come from lean mass — not fat. That means you might hit your goal weight but feel weaker, look less toned, and have a slower metabolism than when you started.

The fix isn't forcing yourself to eat a massive dinner. It's eating small, strategic, protein-rich meals throughout the day — even when nothing sounds good.

GLP Flow tip: Set phone alarms for meals. Seriously. On Wegovy, your hunger signals are muted, so you can't rely on "eating when you're hungry." Set alarms at 8 AM, noon, 3 PM, and 6 PM. When the alarm goes off, eat something with protein — even if it's small.

10 Meals That Go Down Easy When Nothing Sounds Good

These are ranked by how little effort and appetite they require. Start at the top on your hardest days.

1. Protein Shake (The Emergency Meal)

30g protein 200 cal

When you truly cannot eat, sip. A pre-made protein shake is the bare minimum to keep your muscles fed. Keep a few in the fridge at all times. This is your "break glass in case of emergency" meal.

  • 1 pre-made protein shake (Fairlife, Premier Protein, or Orgain)
  • Sip over 30 minutes — don't gulp
  • Add ice if the sweetness is too much

2. Cottage Cheese + Fruit Cup

24g protein 220 cal

Cold, soft, barely requires chewing. The single-serve cottage cheese cups make this a grab-and-go situation. The fruit adds just enough sweetness to make it appealing when everything else sounds terrible.

  • 1 cup low-fat cottage cheese
  • 1/2 cup berries or diced peach
  • Optional: drizzle of honey

3. Greek Yogurt with Nut Butter Swirl

26g protein 280 cal

The nut butter makes this feel like a treat instead of medicine. Almond butter or peanut butter — both work. Stir it in so every bite has some fat and protein together. That combo actually makes it easier to eat when your appetite is flat.

  • 1 cup plain Greek yogurt
  • 1 tbsp almond or peanut butter
  • Sprinkle of granola (optional)

4. Deli Turkey Roll-Ups

22g protein 180 cal

No bread, no cooking, no dishes. Just roll turkey slices around string cheese or avocado. This works because it's something you can eat in small bites over 20 minutes while doing other things. No sitting down for a "meal" required.

  • 4 oz deli turkey (low sodium)
  • 1 stick string cheese or 1/4 avocado
  • Mustard for dipping (optional)

5. Scrambled Eggs (The 3-Minute Meal)

26g protein 250 cal

Soft scrambled eggs are one of the easiest-to-digest proteins that exist. Three eggs, a splash of milk, light on the butter. Add cheese if you can handle it. This is probably the most calorie-efficient cooked meal you can make in under five minutes.

  • 3 large eggs
  • Splash of milk
  • 1 oz shredded cheese
  • Cooking spray or minimal butter
GLP Flow tip: Temperature matters. Most Wegovy users find cold or room-temperature foods easier to stomach than hot meals when appetite is at its lowest. If scrambled eggs sound awful, reach for yogurt, cottage cheese, or a protein shake instead.

6. Tuna Salad on Crackers

28g protein 300 cal

A pouch of tuna, a squeeze of mayo, and some whole grain crackers. It sounds unexciting and that's the point — sometimes boring food is the only food that doesn't trigger aversion. The crackers give it just enough crunch to feel like you're eating a real meal.

  • 1 pouch (2.6 oz) tuna
  • 1 tbsp light mayo or Greek yogurt
  • 6-8 whole grain crackers
  • Salt, pepper, squeeze of lemon

7. Banana + Protein Powder Smoothie

30g protein 320 cal

Drinkable meals are a lifeline on Wegovy. The banana adds potassium and natural sweetness, the spinach sneaks in micronutrients you're probably missing, and you don't have to chew anything. Win across the board.

  • 1 scoop vanilla protein powder
  • 1/2 frozen banana
  • Handful of spinach
  • 1 cup unsweetened almond milk

8. Chicken Soup (Low Volume, High Protein)

25g protein 270 cal

There's a reason people eat soup when they're sick. It's warm, liquid, and goes down without resistance. A broth-based chicken soup with shredded chicken delivers protein without the heavy feeling that solid meals can cause. Store-bought is fine — just check the protein count.

  • 1.5 cups broth-based chicken soup
  • Add extra shredded rotisserie chicken to boost protein
  • Saltines on the side if you want something to crunch

9. Peanut Butter Toast with Hemp Seeds

20g protein 310 cal

Sometimes you need carbs to kickstart your appetite. One slice of toast with peanut butter can actually make you feel hungrier for the next meal — the carbs trigger a small insulin response that wakes up your appetite signals. Hemp seeds add 10g protein per 3 tablespoons.

  • 1 slice whole grain bread, toasted
  • 2 tbsp natural peanut butter
  • 2 tbsp hemp seeds
  • Pinch of sea salt

10. Edamame + Cheese Plate

24g protein 290 cal

A "snack plate" that's secretly a meal. Shelled edamame, a few cubes of cheese, maybe some cherry tomatoes. It works because there's no single dominant flavor — you're grazing on small bites, which feels more manageable than sitting down to a plate of food.

  • 1 cup shelled edamame
  • 1 oz cheddar or gouda, cubed
  • Cherry tomatoes or cucumber slices

The Injection Cycle and Your Appetite

Wegovy works on a weekly injection schedule, and your appetite follows a predictable pattern once you know what to look for:

Days 1-2 after injection (appetite at rock bottom): This is when semaglutide levels peak in your system. Food aversion is strongest. Stick to meals 1-4 on this list — liquids, cold foods, minimal effort. Don't try to cook an elaborate dinner.

Days 3-5 (appetite slowly returning): You'll notice food starts sounding possible again. This is your window to eat slightly larger portions and get your protein up. Meals 5-8 work well here.

Days 6-7 (most normal eating window): Appetite is closest to your pre-Wegovy baseline. Take advantage and eat well. Batch prep protein-rich snacks for the tough days ahead. This is also when to meal prep your cottage cheese cups, roll-ups, and smoothie packs.

GLP Flow tip: Track your protein, not your calories. On Wegovy, obsessing over calorie counts can lead to undereating. Instead, set a daily protein target (0.7-1g per pound of your goal body weight) and focus on hitting that. The calories will take care of themselves.

When to Talk to Your Doctor

Some appetite loss is expected. But call your prescriber if:

  • You consistently eat under 800 calories a day for more than a week
  • You're losing more than 3-4 pounds per week after the first month
  • You feel dizzy, faint, or exhausted even after eating
  • You notice hair thinning or your nails becoming brittle
  • Eating causes pain, not just disinterest

Your doctor may adjust your dose or slow down the dose escalation timeline. That's not a failure — it's smart medicine. The goal is sustainable weight loss, not a crash diet powered by a pharmaceutical.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is it normal to have zero appetite on Wegovy?

Completely normal. Wegovy (semaglutide) works by mimicking GLP-1, a hormone that signals fullness to your brain. Many users report that food just stops sounding appealing, especially in the first 4-6 weeks and after each dose increase. The appetite suppression usually softens over time as your body adjusts, but some degree of reduced hunger is the whole point of the medication.

How many calories should I eat on Wegovy if I'm not hungry?

Most healthcare providers recommend staying above 1,200 calories for women and 1,500 for men, even when appetite is gone. Going below that for extended periods can slow your metabolism, cause muscle loss, and lead to nutrient deficiencies. Focus on protein-dense foods that pack the most nutrition into small portions rather than trying to force large meals.

What happens if I don't eat enough on Wegovy?

Undereating on Wegovy can lead to muscle loss, hair thinning, fatigue, dizziness, and nutrient deficiencies. Your body needs protein to preserve muscle during weight loss — studies show up to 40% of weight lost on GLP-1 medications can come from lean mass if protein intake is too low. Even when food doesn't appeal to you, eating small protein-rich meals every 3-4 hours protects your long-term health.

Does Wegovy appetite loss get better over time?

For most people, yes. The most intense appetite suppression happens during the first few weeks at each new dose level. As your body adjusts, you'll start feeling some hunger signals again — they'll just be quieter than before. Most users find a comfortable middle ground by month 2-3 where they can eat reasonable portions without forcing it.

Should I force myself to eat on Wegovy?

Not force, but yes — eat intentionally even when you're not hungry. Think of it like charging your phone: you don't wait until it dies to plug it in. Schedule small meals every 3-4 hours and focus on protein first. You don't need to clean a plate or eat a big meal. A few bites of high-protein food every few hours is far better than skipping meals entirely.
